Exotic and Flavoured Tea

Exotic and flavoured teas are popular around the world for a reason. There are many studies and research conducted on them which reveal how they're beneficial in preventing diabetes, soothing your digestion, and giving you the mental calmness to contemplate on what you've done for the day. According to the 19th century British Prime Minister, William Ewart Gladstone: "If you are depressed, it will cheer you; If you are excited, it will calm you."

Green Tea

Green tea is known to possess the highest amount of polyphenols out of all the teas. It's the least processed, and it has a compound called 'catechin' which are natural antioxidants that prevent damage from free radicals in the body and lowers the risk of many diseases.

Leaf and Dust Tea

Leaf and dust tea are more finer than Fannings and have the leaves pulverized into a fine powder. Leaf and dust tea are rarely sold as powders but as tea bags and you won't get them in loose-form in most places.

Tea Bags

Tea bags made their commercial debut in 1908, and the reasoning behind their patent was convenience. There's no clean up involved when you use tea bags and the time required to steep is shorter, thus giving you those advantages.

Health Benefits of Drinking Tea

Thinking of getting into the habit of drinking tea? Here's a list of health benefits you can expect.
1. They Have Antioxidants Antioxidants are what you need to keep looking young and prevent catching diseases. When you drink tea in combination with a healthy diet, you do your body a huge favour
2. Contains Little to No Caffeine
Caffeine taxes your adrenals, but you'll be glad to know that herbal teas contain no caffeine. Plus, some teas like the Chicory Root Tea are known to act as a prebiotic which benefits your gut.
3. Could Help Lower Your Risk of Heart Attack and Stroke
There's a lot of evidence that points to the fact that drinking tea long-term leads to a reduced incidence of heart attacks and stroke.
4. Boosts Your Metabolism
If you're trying to lose weight, you'll be glad to know that tea boosts your metabolism. This means you burn more calories even while you rest, keeping those pounds off for good.
5. Protects Your Bone Health
Certain types of tea like teas made from Moringa leaves are known to contain high amounts of calcium (even more than milk!) This helps protect your bone healthy by maintaining its density, making teas a superfood in their own right.

Tea Recipes You Can Try

There's a lot you can do when it comes to enjoying your tea. Whether you just want to steep a tea bag in hot water or give a creative spin, try out these recipes, and you won't be left disappointed:
1. Detox Tea
This tea recipe will help you flush out those toxins and make your skin glow
Ingredients
6 glasses of water
10 to 12 mint leaves
3 Green Teabags
1 lemon (medium)
Total Time Required
11 mins
How to Prepare
Add the six glasses of water to a pan and bring it to a boil
Turn off the flame and mix in 3 green teabags sachets
Cover with a lid for 5 minutes
Remove and let it cool
Strain the tea and add in the mint leaves
Squeeze the lemon juice and mix it in
Consume immediately or refrigerate in a glass jar for later use
2. Ginger Tea
Ginger tea is a classic and a must-try recipe.
Ingredients
3 to 4 glasses of water
3 cardamoms (medium)
1 ginger
1 tablespoon of tea leaves
1 cup of milk
Total Time Required
15 mins
How to Prepare
Add water in a pan and mix in the cardamoms and ginger
Bring it to a boil and wait for a minute
Add in sugar, tea leaves, and boil for a few minutes
Remove from heat and cover with a lid
Stir well and let the mixture sit for 2 minutes
Strain the tea and transfer to another vessel
Add milk and boil again
Let it cool a bit and serve immediately
